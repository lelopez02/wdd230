Welcome to Buenos Aires

Demographic Data

The population of the City of Buenos Aires is about 2.9 million inhabitants. It is known to be one of the most densely populated urban areas in Latin America. It is estimated that about 19% of the population is under the age of 15, about 66% is in the 15 to 64 age range, and about 15% is 65 or older.

The Obelisk of Buenos Aires is a historical monument considered an icon of the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires, the capital of Argentina. It was built in 1936 on the occasion of the fourth centenary of the so-called first foundation of Buenos Aires by Pedro de Mendoza.

The Obelisk, was the main stage in the party that started the 2018 Youth Olympic Games, when it became a giant vertical screen where with an amazing game of lights and sounds a countdown was performed.

Floralis Generic

The Floralis Gen√©rica is a metallic sculpture located in the Plaza de las Naciones Unidas, Avenida Figueroa Alcorta, in the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires. It was inaugurated on April 13, 2002.

One of the features of the flower is an electrical system that automatically opens and closes the petals depending on the time of day. During the night, the flower closes, emanating a red glow from its interior to be reborn open in the morning of the following day. This same mechanism is the one that closes the flower in strong winds, emulating the process of photonastia of nature.

It opens every morning at 8 a.m. and closes at sunset, on a schedule that changes according to the season of the year. When it was inaugurated, its petals did not close due to technical problems that were solved two months later.

Puerto Madero

Puerto Madero is one of the forty-eight neighborhoods in which the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires (CABA), capital of Argentina, is divided. Its location near the eastern part of the city, its extensive area and its view of the river make this district one of the most valued in Buenos Aires.

In the last years numerous residential towers of great category were built and are in construction, standing out the Towers El Faro, River View Towers, Le Parc Puerto Madero Towers, Mulieris Towers, Repsol-YPF Tower, or Renoir Towers, all of them of more than 130 meters high, reaching 175 meters in the case of the Renoir and they are the biggest in the country.

The construction of the Alvear Tower, which will be one of the tallest towers in South America and the tallest in the country with its 235 meters of height, is currently being finalized.

Japanese Garden

Surrounded by the Palermo Woods, the Japanese Garden is a corner of Buenos Aires. It is the largest Japanese-style garden outside of Japan. Walking through bonsais, azaleas, kokedamas, orchids and concrete lanterns is an unforgettable pleasure. It also has a Chashitsu (tea house): a space specially built to carry out the traditional Japanese tea ceremony. Ornamented with traditional elements, whose interior was imported from Japan, some of its components date back more than a hundred years. Its exquisite interior is handcrafted. An art room and a restaurant complete the visit.

In the park there is also a building that houses a cultural center; a Japanese cuisine restaurant; a nursery where you can buy plants (bonsai) and food for the fish in the lake; and a store with Japanese handicrafts.

El Planetario

The Planetarium is the main astronomy outreach center in the city. It has a hemispherical projection room with three hundred and sixty reclining seats and a dome of twenty meters in diameter where about 8,900 stars, planets and satellites of the universe are reproduced, thanks to an impressive equipment (unique in Latin America) containing six Sky-Skan projectors with 8K resolution, which means that the dome contains 38 million pixels.

Its five-story building also houses a museum and a small secondary projection room, as well as a collection of meteorites from northern Argentina.
